Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 23, 2026

Overview

RedactFlow is designed to help users blur, pixelate, or cover sensitive areas in photos and videos before sharing. Privacy is a core product principle.

Data Collection

RedactFlow does not collect personal data, does not create user accounts, and does not include analytics, advertising SDKs, or third-party trackers.

Photo and Video Access

The app requests access to the Photos library only so you can import selected photos or videos and save edited exports back to your library.

On-Device Processing

All redaction and export processing is performed locally on your iPhone. Media is not uploaded to external servers or cloud services by RedactFlow.

Data Sharing

RedactFlow does not sell, share, or disclose your media or personal information to third parties.

Data Retention

RedactFlow does not maintain a remote database of user content. Imported media remains in your Photos library unless you remove it. Temporary local files may be created during editing or export and are only used for app functionality.

Children's Privacy

RedactFlow does not knowingly collect personal information from children.

Policy Changes

If this Privacy Policy changes, the updated version will be posted on this page with a revised effective date.

Before Contacting Support

  • Make sure your iPhone is running a supported iOS version.
  • Check whether Photos access is set to Full Access or Limited Access.
  • Try reopening the app if an import or export action appears stuck.

Helpful Details to Include

  • Your iPhone model and iOS version
  • The RedactFlow app version
  • Whether the issue happened with a photo or a video
  • Whether Photos permission was Full Access or Limited Access
  • A short description of what happened

Common Support Topics

  • Photos permission and limited library access
  • Importing photos or videos into the editor
  • Saving exports back to Photos
  • Blur, pixelate, or black bar behavior
